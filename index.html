The Elaboration Likelihood Model (ELM): When consumers fear a destination, they dismiss corporate ads via the "central route" of critical evaluation. Influencer content bypasses this entirely — the brain processes a creator's real-time experience as indisputable peer evidence, not branded messaging.
Parasocial Trust: Followers perceive influencers as intimate peers, not corporate entities. When a trusted creator travels to Saudi Arabia and broadcasts their safety, followers experience a vicarious transfer of confidence. This psychological mechanism is documented in peer-reviewed research and has no equivalent in traditional advertising.
UGC vs Official Sources: Research confirms that user-generated and influencer content is perceived as vastly more reliable than official tourism websites, travel agents, and mass media. The authenticity gap between "a tourism board saying Saudi is safe" and "a creator you follow showing you Riyadh on their phone" is immeasurable.
